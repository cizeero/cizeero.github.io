# Tawhid

God is one. Indivisible. In a separate category of his own. There is no sense of plurarility here.

# Wajib al Wujud

The whole existence inclines upon God. Anything other than God's existence is contingent, it can or it cannot. But God has to exist for existence to make sense. God is the condition for "being" itself.

## Mu'tazilites

A set of believers that explain that all the properties assigned to him, are not properties but identical to him itself. All knowing, immortal, eternal. These are not properties of God, but God itself.

> God's will is the ultimate explanation: things are good because God wills them, not willed because they are good

# God as the ultimate chase

As an artist, the chase itself it the meaning, for its own sake. The only truly endless chase is the chase of God, its inexhaustible. Al Samad, the one towards everything turns, so should our chase be. God is a name for inexhaustibility. Without constraint, there's no craft, only vague intention. Dunya is the constraint, the resistance that brings out my art. Dunya becomes a trap only when you cling to it rather than work it.

# Ihsan

The perfect alignment between what I believe and the hadees. Ihsan, the act of beauty and perfection showing in both deed and action. The act of worshipping to god as though they see him, but knowing very well that he is seeing them. The indomitable spirit to not do things imperfectly, to not give up. Ihsan has the same root word as "Husn" meaning beauty, or the word "Hasan" meaning good.

> Is the reward of ihsan anything but ihsan?

When you live your life as though you are under the watch of something infinite, your life first transitions into a life of fear, which is a lower station, finally reaching to ihsaan where you cannot do something that's _ugly_.

As though you see him, Knowing that he sees you. It is not about living the bare minimum, virtue ethics align with this philosophy, it is about what would a perfect person do? Ihsan isn't compartmentalized to "worship" it's a mode of engaging everything. Applying Ihsan in your lives is to apple Itqan, the precision and mastery of doing your job. Whatever you do, you do it as though this is your art. Refusing to let your physical existence a background noise, and to focus on some of the most basic acts such as drinking water with your mind.

You practice Muraqaba, the act of sitting in isolation, as though you are being observed by an entity of infinite wisdom. You practice Tafakkur, the contemplation of deep idealogies such as morality, the nature of time etc. There are no shortcuts in practicing ihsan. The indomitable spirit isn't a single heroic act but the refusal, day after day, to lapse into carelessness.

Arete, sitting in the same context as ihsan, the practice of execellence. You dont force yourself, but become the person for who this becomes natural enough. The same holds for prosoche, the term that Marcus Aurelias talked about, the act of doing everything as if it were your last act. Traditions diverge on the metaphysics aspect of it, but they converge in the result, the feeling of acting upon these ideas. The philosophy of learning arete is equivalent to learning instruments. First you start with a arbitrary believe system, and the mind takes us towards anything. You use Enkrateia, a form of self resistance to shape your habitual behaviour. Over time your behaviour gets shaped into pursuing arete, thus feeling Eudamonia.

The clear paralell to resistance that is drawn in Quran is nafs, the self or the ego. There are stages to it, and those stages are slowly overcome as also described by Aristotle.
