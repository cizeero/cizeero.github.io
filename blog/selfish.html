Building Product for Selfish Reasons

Blog Home

The entire mentality of building or solving a problem has always been about "Oh this will help the world or someone else" or "This will make money for me" and don't get me wrong, this works for a lot of people. This is true for not just products, but about other stuff as well, like this essay, or a home purchase, or even research. That mentality does not work for me. I realized that the only way I work on something hard is creation of non-existent in the form I desire. My curiosity is aligned in understanding how its existence will look like, I want to detail it. I do everything to facilitate the existence of that picture. I want to know how this essay will turn out, or how a bot playing Minecraft would look like, things of this sort where other people do not have a role. I need to put that curiosity at the center & I have to get the help of the entire world in making that happen. In research that help is reading the existing literature, or running experiments, or talking to people & collaborating on all scales. I don't have to do this because it's going to help me in my life, but because I want my answers, I want to see how they look.

I now extend this theory in building products, writing novels, solving tough problems. There are a lot of things that I wish they existed, and I have no choice but do things that can make that happen by getting all the help I need, and this help can arrive in all forms. In this picture, customers are no longer the people that just support your endeavour of building, but they are a set of people that are going to give you ideas on how to improve it, to help you guide these ideas into existence. Your single brain will not do it, I need all the help I can get, because I alone cannot imagine all the possibilities, or perspectives, and I don't want to.

I have been getting the order of things completely wrong, that's because everyone is getting it wrong. Building to solve other people's problems is stupid, trying to fit everyone's taste is stupid. Ditch what people ask. No, I have to synthesize what they ask into ideas that can help see my answers into existence. Get close to my answers, it's the only thing that matters. I have my selfish reasons to build & research, it might help the world, but I definitely don't want to think about that. Your customers will reap your creations only when you have created them with your true heart, don't by sycophantic, do not betray yourself.

This essay are my thoughts, and they are unpolished, unfinished. You and I will benefit from this essay if you come back to me and discuss about it.
