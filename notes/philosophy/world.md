# Dunya

Dunya is a philosophically loaded term, which means nearer or lower. The current world is not evil, but temporary, derivative. The Quran describes dunya as mata' al-ghurur, "the enjoyment of delusion". _zuhd_ the islamic version of aescetism is the idealogy of not denouncing the worldly pleasures, but being indifferent to them. Ibn Sina similarly held that the soul's ultimate happiness is intellectual — the contemplation of eternal truths. In this sense, dunya is a means to an end, literally. Your happiness starts from here, in the contemplation of eternal truths. Underlying all of this is the concept of dunya as ibtila (trial/test). Human life is a moral examination: will you see through the glitter of dunya to the deeper reality? Will you orient yourself toward the permanent or the passing?

God lives outside time, time is referred as zaman, and the closeness to god, the eternity is beyond this. The test is to believe in this eternity, contemplate the true nature of dunya, and exercise in the intellectual thoughts. Rabia wanted to burn paradise, and extinguish hell so that people follow God for its own sake, rather than for a reward. The highest spiritual station is when the pursuit itself becomes indistinguishable from fulfillment.
